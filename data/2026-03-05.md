<details>

### [2] [Proving and Computing: The Infinite Pigeonhole Principle and Countable Choice](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04006)
*Zena M. Ariola,Paul Downen,Hugo Herbelin*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 论文探讨了在经典推理背景下结构共递归的表达能力，特别是结合callcc操作符，展示了这种组合如何实现有趣的流处理算法，并应用于无限鸽巢原理和可数选择公理。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 结构共递归虽然在Haskell、Rocq、Agda等语言中得到支持，但通常被认为比结构递归更高级，主要在纯函数式编程中研究。本文旨在展示在经典推理背景下不同结构共递归概念的表达能力。

Method: 研究共迭代和共递归与经典callcc操作符的结合，callcc提供了经典推理的计算解释。这种组合使得能够设计有趣的流处理算法。具体应用包括：1）基于控制和共递归的无限鸽巢原理证明；2）使用共迭代单独证明终止的可数选择公理实现。

Result: 展示了混合共递归和控制操作的能力：1）提供了无限鸽巢原理的共递归、基于控制的证明，与Escardó和Oliva在Agda中的延续传递证明进行比较；2）实现了可数选择公理，与通常依赖外部终止证明的一般递归延续传递实现不同，本文方法仅通过共迭代就能证明终止。

Conclusion: 结构共递归与经典控制操作符（如callcc）的结合具有强大的表达能力，能够实现优雅的算法设计，特别是在处理无限或大数据集合时。这种方法不仅提供了新的证明技术，还能更好地处理经典推理中的构造性问题。

Abstract: Structural recursion is a common technique used by programmers in modern languages and is taught to introductory computer science students. But what about its dual, structural corecursion? Structural corecursion is an elegant technique, supported in languages like Haskell and proof assistants such as Rocq or Agda.  It enables the design of compositional algorithms by decoupling the generation and consumption of potentially infinite or large data collections. Despite these strengths, structural corecursion is generally considered more advanced than structural recursion and is primarily studied in the context of pure functional programming.
  Our aim is to illustrate the expressive power of different notions of structural corecursion in the presence of classical reasoning. More specifically, we study coiteration and corecursion combined with the classical callcc operator, which provides a computational interpretation of classical reasoning. This combination enables interesting stream-processing algorithms. As an application, we present a corecursive, control-based proof of the Infinite Pigeonhole Principle and compare it with the continuation-passing proof of Escardó and Oliva in Agda. To further demonstrate the power of mixing corecursion and control, we give an implementation of the Axiom of Countable Choice. In contrast to the usual continuation-passing implementations of this axiom, which rely on general recursion whose termination is established externally, our approach justifies termination by coiteration alone.

</details>


### [3] [A Core Calculus for Type-safe Product Lines of C Programs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04013)
*Ferruccio Damiani,Daisuke Kimura,Luca Paolini,Makoto Tatsuta*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 提出轻量级C语言核心演算LC，扩展为带预处理器指令的CLC，并为其设计类型系统保证预处理后程序类型安全


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 为C语言预处理器提供形式化基础，确保预处理后的程序类型安全，同时可用于教学目的

Method: 1) 定义LC作为ANSI C子集的形式化核心演算；2) 扩展为带预处理器指令的CLC；3) 为CLC设计类型系统保证预处理后程序类型正确

Result: 建立了C语言预处理器的形式化框架，确保预处理生成的程序都是良类型的C程序

Abstract: In this paper we: (1) propose Lightweight C (LC), namely a core calculus that formalizes a proper subset of the ANSI C without preprocessor directives; (2) define Colored LC (CLC), namely LC endowed with ANSI C preprocessor directives; and (3) define a type system for CLC that guarantees that all programs to be generated by the C preprocessor are well-typed C programs. We believe that the simple formalization provided by CLC could be useful also for teaching purposes.
  Stefano Berardi spent most of his academic career at the Department of Computer Science of the University of Turin, where he conducts outstanding research on the logical foundations of computer science and on type-based program analyses. Over the years, he taught many courses, from BSc courses on programming with C to PhD courses on program analysis. Therefore, this paper fully falls within Stefano Berardi's research and teaching activities.

### [4] [SpotIt+: Verification-based Text-to-SQL Evaluation with Database Constraints](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04334)
*Rocky Klopfenstein,Yang He,Andrew Tremante,Yuepeng Wang,Nina Narodytska,Haoze Wu*

Main category: cs.DB

TL;DR: SpotIt+是一个开源工具，通过有界等价验证评估Text-to-SQL系统，通过搜索区分生成SQL和真实SQL的数据库实例来发现差异。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统基于测试的评估方法可能遗漏SQL查询间的差异，需要更有效的方法来发现Text-to-SQL系统中的实际相关差异。

Method: 结合基于规则的规范挖掘和LLM验证的约束挖掘流程，主动搜索区分两个SQL查询的数据库实例。

Result: 在BIRD数据集上，挖掘的约束使SpotIt+能生成更现实的区分数据库，同时高效发现标准测试评估遗漏的大量差异。

Conclusion: SpotIt+通过约束挖掘和等价验证，为Text-to-SQL系统评估提供了更有效的方法，能发现实际相关差异。

Abstract: We present SpotIt+, an open-source tool for evaluating Text-to-SQL systems via bounded equivalence verification. Given a generated SQL query and the ground truth, SpotIt+ actively searches for database instances that differentiate the two queries. To ensure that the generated counterexamples reflect practically relevant discrepancies, we introduce a constraint-mining pipeline that combines rule-based specification mining over example databases with LLM-based validation. Experimental results on the BIRD dataset show that the mined constraints enable SpotIt+ to generate more realistic differentiating databases, while preserving its ability to efficiently uncover numerous discrepancies between generated and gold SQL queries that are missed by standard test-based evaluation.

</details>

### [5] [Lambdas at the Far Edge: a Tale of Flying Lambdas and Lambdas on Wheels](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04008)
*Giorgio Audrito,Daniele Bortoluzzi,Ferruccio Damiani,Giordano Scarso,Gianluca Torta,Andrea Basso,Monica Cochi,Lorenzo Gusman,Lorenzo Comba,Paolo Gay,Paola Dal Zovo,Giada Galati,Francesco Gallo,Aljaž Grdadolnik,Massimo Pescarollo,Paola Pisano*

Main category: cs.DC

TL;DR: 本文介绍了eXchange Calculus (XC)作为聚合编程的基础模型，以及其C++实现FCPP库，该库可用于编程分布式边缘设备的集体行为。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 聚合编程需要一种能够编程分布式设备集体行为的范式，特别是在网络远边缘环境中。XC作为一种基础模型，通过交换操作符提供隐式通信机制，为这类系统提供了理论基础。

Method: 提出eXchange Calculus (XC)，一种扩展了交换操作符的类型化lambda演算，并实现了C++库FCPP。FCPP程序本质上是类型化lambda项，支持在边缘设备上部署。

Result: 开发了FCPP库和工具链，已成功部署在远边缘网络设备上，包括漫游车和无人机等实际应用场景。

Conclusion: XC和FCPP为聚合编程提供了坚实的基础模型和实用工具，能够有效编程分布式边缘设备的集体行为，具有实际应用价值。

Abstract: Aggregate Programming (AP) is a paradigm for programming the collective behaviour of sets of distributed devices, possibly situated at the network far edge, by relying on asynchronous proximity-based interactions. The eXchange Calculus (XC), a recently proposed foundational model for AP, is essentially a typed lambda calculus extended with an operator (the exchange operator) providing an implicit communication mechanism between neighbour devices. This paper provides a gentle introduction to XC and to its implementation as a C++ library, called FCPP. The FCPP library and toolchain has been mainly developed at the Department of Computer Science of the University of Turin, where Stefano Berardi spent most of his academic career conducting outstanding research about logical foundation of computer science and transmitting his passion for research to students and young researchers, often exploiting typed lambda calculi. An FCCP program is essentially a typed lambda term, and FCPP has been used to write code that has been deployed on devices at the far edge of the network, including rovers and (soon) Uncrewed Aerial Vehicles (UAVs); hence the title of the paper.

</details>
