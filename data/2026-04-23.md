### [1] [Going MLIR-native: Demonstrating a Future for DSL compilers on a NumPy-like Example](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19906)
*Karl F. A. Friebel,Jascha A. Ohlmann,Jeronimo Castrillon*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种完全基于MLIR的NumPy风格DSL，用于数值张量核的卸载。该DSL实现了所有前端操作和语义分析，并提出了新的方言无关的MLIR类型检查器。通过并行优先的降级方案，连接数据流方言实现无缝卸载，在气象建模和CFD领域表现良好。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 通用语言编译器在特定应用领域的表现不如DSL编译器，但DSL编译器领域缺乏统一的框架和生态系统，导致大量重复工作、维护困难，大多DSL难以达到"生产就绪"水平。MLIR的出现为解决这一问题提供了契机。

Method: 1) 构建NumPy风格DSL，完全MLIR原生实现；2) 提出方言无关的MLIR类型检查器（开源首创）；3) 实现并行优先的降级方案，连接MLIR数据流方言实现卸载。

Result: 该方法在气象建模和计算流体动力学（CFD）的真实Fortran用例中表现良好。

Conclusion: MLIR有潜力像LLVM对通用工具链的影响一样，推动DSL编译器的整合与发展。本文的MLIR原生DSL方案证明了这一方向的可行性。

Abstract: Compilers for general-purpose languages have been shown to be at a disadvantage when it comes to specialized application domains as opposed to their Domain-Specific Language (DSL) counterparts. However, the field of DSL compilers features little consolidation in terms of compiler frameworks and adjacent software ecosystems. As a result, considerable work is duplicated, lost to maintenance issues, or remains undiscovered, and most DSLs are never considered "production-ready". One notable development is the introduction of the Multi-Level Intermediate Representation (MLIR), which promises a similar impact on DSL compilers as LLVM had on general-purpose tooling.
  In this work, we present a NumPy-like DSL made for offloading numeric tensor kernels that is entirely MLIR-native. In a first for open-source, it implements all frontend actions and semantic analyses directly within MLIR. Most notably, this is made possible by our new dialect-agnostic MLIR type checker, created for the future of DSLs in MLIR. We implement a simple, yet effective, parallel-first lowering scheme that connects our language to another MLIR dataflow dialect for seamless offloading. We show that our approach performs well in real-world use cases from the domain of weather modeling and Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) in Fortran.

### [2] [Automatic Code and Test Generation of Smart Contracts from Coordination Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20507)
*Elvis Konjoh Selabi,Maurizio Murgia,António Ravara,Emilio Tuosto*

TL;DR: 提出了一种用于去中心化协调的形式化方法，支持动态角色、数据驱动转换和外部协调接口，并实现了包含模型验证、代码生成（Solidity）和测试合成的工具链，通过智能合约中的协调模式验证了其实用性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 分布式系统中去中心化协调缺乏形式化的规范和实现方法，尤其是在智能合约场景下，现有方法难以支持动态角色、数据驱动转换等高级特性。

Method: 建立形式化模型捕获动态角色、数据驱动转换和外部协调接口；开发工具链支持模型验证、Solidity代码生成（可扩展至其他语言）和自动化测试合成。

Result: 通过建模和实现智能合约中的若干协调模式，展示了该方法的表达能力和实用性。

Abstract: We propose a formal approach for specifying and implementing decentralised coordination in distributed systems, with a focus on smart contracts. Our model captures dynamic roles, data-driven transitions, and external coordination interfaces, enabling high-level reasoning about decentralised workflows. We implement a toolchain that supports formal model validation, code generation for Solidity (our framework is extendable to other smart contract languages), and automated test synthesis. Although our implementation targets blockchain platforms, the methodology is platform-agnostic and may generalise to other service-oriented and distributed architectures. We demonstrate the expressiveness and practicality of the approach by modelling and realising some coordination patterns in smart contracts.

### [3] [Extending Contract Verification for Parallel Programming Models to Fortran](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20410)
*Yussur Mustafa Oraji,Christian Bischof*

Main category: cs.DC

TL;DR: 本文扩展了契约验证框架CoVer，使其支持Fortran语言，实现了跨C/C++和Fortran的并行程序静态与动态分析，并在保持精度的同时比MUST工具更高效。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有并行编程模型正确性检查工具多局限于C/C++、特定库实现或特定错误类型，缺乏对Fortran等语言的通用支持。

Method: 在CoVer框架基础上，适配了Fortran语言的契约定义，并修改分析流程使其支持C/C++和Fortran程序的多语言静态与动态分析。

Result: 增强版CoVer保持了原有分析精度，发现了MPI-BugBench测试框架中的漏洞，且Fortran版本的效率显著优于MUST工具。

Conclusion: CoVer成功扩展至Fortran，实现了跨语言并行程序验证，兼具通用性与高效性。

Abstract: High-performance computing often relies on parallel programming models such as MPI for distributed-memory systems. While powerful, these models are prone to subtle programming errors, leading to development of multiple correctness checking tools. However, these are often limited to C/C++ codes, tied to specific library implementations, or restricted to certain error classes. Building on our prior work with CoVer, a generic, contract-based verification framework for parallel programming models, we extend CoVer's applicability to Fortran, enabling static and dynamic analysis across multiple programming languages. We adapted language-specific contract definitions and modified the analyses to support both C/C++ and Fortran programs. Our evaluation demonstrates that the enhanced version preserves CoVer's analysis accuracy and even revealed a bug in the MPI-BugBench testing framework, underscoring the effectiveness of the approach. The Fortran port of CoVer turns out to be substantially more efficient than the state-of-the-art tool MUST, while maintaining generality across languages.

</details>

### [4] [Worst-Case Optimal GPU Datalog](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20073)
*Yihao Sun,Kunting Qi,Thomas Gilray,Sidharth Kumar,Kristopher Micinski*

Main category: cs.DB

TL;DR: 本文提出SRDatalog，首个基于最坏情况最优连接（WCOJ）的GPU Datalog引擎。通过扁平列式存储、两阶段确定性内存分配、直方图引导的负载均衡等技术，解决了GPU上WCOJ因键倾斜导致的SIMT负载不均问题，在真实程序分析任务上实现21-47倍加速。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有GPU Datalog引擎基于二元连接（binary joins），在处理DOOP、ddisasm等生产系统中的复杂多路查询时，二元分解会导致AGM界的时间空间渐近爆炸，引发OOM错误。而WCOJ虽能避免此问题，但其逐属性交集方式在SIMT硬件上因键倾斜（key skew）导致严重的流多处理器（SM）负载不均衡。

Method: 1) 扁平列式存储和两阶段确定性内存分配，避免OOM和索引重建开销；2) 根级直方图引导的负载均衡，缓解键倾斜问题；3) 结构性辅助关系拆分，隐藏硬件停顿；4) 流对齐规则复用（stream-aligned rule multiplexing）。

Result: 在真实程序分析工作负载上，SRDatalog相比现有GPU Datalog引擎获得21倍到47倍的几何平均加速。

Conclusion: SRDatalog首次将WCOJ成功移植到GPU上，克服了SIMT硬件下的负载不均衡问题，证明了GPU上使用WCOJ处理复杂Datalog查询的可行性和高效性。

Abstract: Datalog is a declarative logic-programming language used for complex analytic reasoning workloads such as program analysis and graph analytics. Datalog's popularity is due to its unique price-point, marrying logic-defined specification with the potential for massive data parallelism. While traditional engines are CPU-based, the memory-bound nature of Datalog has led to increasing interest in leveraging GPUs. These engines beat CPU-based engines by operationalizing iterated relational joins via SIMT-friendly join algorithms. Unfortunately, all existing GPU Datalog engines are built on binary joins, which are inadequate for the complex multi-way queries arising in production systems such as DOOP and ddisasm. For these queries, binary decomposition can incur the AGM bound asymptotic blowup in time and space, leading to OOM failures regardless of join order. Worst-Case Optimal Joins (WCOJ) avoid this blowup, but their attribute-at-a-time intersections map poorly to SIMT hardware under key skew, causing severe load imbalance across Streaming Multiprocessors (SMs). We present SRDatalog, the first GPU Datalog engine based on WCOJ. SRDatalog uses flat columnar storage and two-phase deterministic memory allocation to avoid the OOM failures of binary joins and the index-rebuild overheads of static WCOJ systems. To mitigate skew and hide hardware stalls, SRDatalog further employs root-level histogram-guided load balancing, structural helper-relation splitting, and stream-aligned rule multiplexing. On real-world program-analysis workloads, SRDatalog achieves geometric-mean speedups of 21x to 47x.

</details>
