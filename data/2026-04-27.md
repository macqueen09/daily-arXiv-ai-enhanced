### [1] [Causality and Semantic Separation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22041)
*Anna Zhang,Qinglan Luo,London Bielicke,Eunice Jun,Adam Chlipala*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文为科学实验设计的形式化验证提供了新视角，将d-分离条件与安全理论中的非干扰概念建立了精确的语义对应关系，并在Rocq证明助手中完成了机械化验证。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 科学实验设计中常出现混淆变量未被控制的错误，而因果推断中的d-分离条件虽能解决此问题但其正确性缺乏严格的语义学证明。作者希望从程序语言社区的标准出发，为d-分离提供可证明可靠的语义基础。

Method: 作者将d-分离这一图论条件与安全理论中的非干扰概念进行类比，提出了一种新的语义定义，并通过Rocq证明助手完成了机械化定理证明，表明两者在语义上精确等价。

Result: 证明了d-分离条件与基于非干扰概念的语义定义完全一致，为d-分离的正确性提供了独立于概率假设的结构化语义基础。每个实验质量自动测试都对应一种反驳世界建模假设的方法。

Conclusion: 该工作桥接了编程语言理论与因果推断领域，为实验设计的自动化验证提供了严格的理论支撑，使得传统上直觉性的d-分离条件获得了形式化的语义解释。

Abstract: The design of scientific experiments deserves its own variation of formal verification to catch cases where scientists made important mistakes, such as forgetting to take confounding variables into account. One of the most fundamental underpinnings of science is causality, or what it means for interventions in the world to cause other outcomes, as formalized by computer scientists like Judea Pearl. However, these ideas had not previously been made rigorous to the standards of the programming-languages community, where one expects a (syntactic) program analysis to be proved sound with respect to a natural semantics. In the domain of causality, as the relevant "program analysis," we focus on $d$-separation, a classic condition on graphs that can be used to decide when the design of an experiment controls for sufficiently many confounding variables, even though the reason that this condition works is often unintuitive. Our central result (mechanized in Rocq) is that $d$-separation exactly coincides with a novel semantic definition inspired by noninterference from the theory of security. This characterization provides a structural semantic foundation for $d$-separation and helps explain why the graph-theoretic condition is correct, independently of probabilistic assumptions. For each given automated test on the quality of an experiment design, our theorem justifies an associated method for falsifying the world-modeling hypothesis behind the experiment.

### [2] [From Monolithic to Compositional: A Compositional Operational Semantics for Crystality](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22210)
*Ziyun Xu,Hao Wang,Meng Sun*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文为面向并行EVM的智能合约语言Crystality提出了组合操作语义，将系统分解为引擎组件和全局组件，显式表达并行执行结构，并通过双模拟定理证明其与原语义等价。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 并行执行是提升区块链可扩展性的关键方法，但智能合约语言在并行环境下缺乏形式语义，导致严谨推理困难。

Method: 将原有的单体语义分解为引擎组件和全局组件，提出组合操作语义；基于编码解码函数，通过事务级双模拟和代码级双模拟证明语义等价性。

Result: 证明了组合语义的关键结构性质（局部性、全局隔离、独立局部步骤的强交换性），并证明了组合语义与原语义在事务级和代码级上的语义等价性。

Conclusion: 提出的组合语义为并行区块链上的智能合约提供了严谨的形式化基础，便于推理和验证。

Abstract: Parallel execution has become a key approach to improving blockchain scalability, but the lack of formal semantics for smart contract languages in such settings makes rigorous reasoning difficult. Crystality is a smart contract language designed for parallel EVMs, supporting scoped state and asynchronous relay across execution engines. This paper introduces a compositional operational semantics for Crystality. Unlike the original monolithic semantics, the new semantics decomposes the system into engine components and a global component, making the structure of parallel execution explicit. The compositional formulation enables simple proofs of key structural properties, including locality, global isolation, and strong commutativity of independent local steps. Furthermore, we prove that the compositional semantics is semantically equivalent to the original one via a transaction-level bisimulation theorem based on encoding and decoding functions between configurations, and two code-level bisimulation theorems for local and global execution.

</details>


### [3] [Ownership Refinement Types for Pointer Arithmetic and Nested Arrays](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22361)
*Yusuke Fujiwara,Yusuke Matsushita,Kohei Suenaga,Atsushi Igarashi*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文扩展了Tanaka等人的类型系统，通过泛化所有权概念以支持外层数组索引，实现了对嵌套数组（如矩阵）的验证，并证明了系统的可靠性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Tanaka等人的类型系统虽然支持数组和指针运算的验证，但无法处理嵌套数组（如矩阵）这类实际数据结构，限制了其实用性。

Method: 泛化所有权概念，使其能够引用外层数组的索引，从而支持嵌套数组；基于该类型系统实现了验证器。

Result: 该验证器能够成功验证涉及嵌套数组操作的程序正确性，这些程序超出了Tanaka等人系统的处理能力。

Conclusion: 通过泛化所有权概念，成功将Tanaka等人的类型系统扩展至支持嵌套数组，并证明了扩展后系统的可靠性，扩展了程序验证的适用范围。

Abstract: Tanaka et al. proposed a type system for verifying functional correctness properties of programs that use arrays and pointer arithmetic. Their system extends ConSORT -- a type system combining fractional ownership and refinement types for imperative program verification -- with support for pointer arithmetic. Their idea was to extend fractional ownership so that it can depend on an array index. Their formulation, however, does not handle nested arrays, which are essential for representing practical data structures such as matrices. We extend Tanaka et al.'s type system to support nested arrays by generalizing the notion of ownership to be able to refer to the indices of the outer arrays and prove the soundness of the extended type system. We have implemented a verifier based on the proposed type system and demonstrated that it can verify the correctness of programs that manipulate nested arrays, which were beyond the reach of Tanaka et al.

</details>

### [4] [BLAST: Benchmarking LLMs with ASP-based Structured Testing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22306)
*Manuel Alejandro Borroto Santana,Erica Coppolillo,Francesco Calimeri,Giuseppe Manco,Simona Perri,Francesco Ricca*

Main category: cs.LO

TL;DR: 本文提出BLAST，首个专门用于评估LLM生成ASP代码能力的基准测试方法和数据集，包含两种新颖的语义指标，并通过8个最新LLM在10个经典ASP图问题上的实验验证了其有效性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 目前LLM在声明式编程范式（如Answer Set Programming, ASP）上的表现尚未得到充分研究，缺乏专门的评估基准和度量指标。

Method: 构建了BLAST基准测试框架，包括专用数据集和两种面向ASP代码生成的新型语义评估指标，选取8个SOTA LLM在10个经典图相关ASP问题上进行实验。

Result: 通过8个LLM在10个ASP图问题上的实证评估，展示了各模型在ASP代码生成任务上的性能差异。

Abstract: Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable performance across a broad spectrum of tasks, including natural language understanding, dialogue systems, and code generation. Despite evident progress, less attention has been paid to their effectiveness in handling declarative paradigms such as Answer Set Programming (ASP), to date. In this paper we introduce BLAST: The first dedicated benchmarking methodology and associated dataset for evaluating the accuracy of LLMs in generating ASP code. BLAST provides a structured evaluation framework featuring two novel semantic metrics tailored to ASP code generation. The paper presents the results of an empirical evaluation involving ten well-established graph-related problems from the ASP literature and a diverse set of eight state-of-the-art LLMs.

### [5] [Kernel Contracts: A Specification Language for ML Kernel Correctness Across Heterogeneous Silicon](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22032)
*Cooper Veit*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种用于描述机器学习内核合约的形式化规范语言，包含八个组成部分，并定义了十二种合约类别。通过三个真实案例验证，该框架能将非正式诊断映射为可测量的合约违规行为，为跨平台内核行为一致性提供规范性参考。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 当前ML内核隐式携带计算合约但很少明文记录，导致跨平台结果不一致时缺乏正式仲裁手段。已有实证工作仅测量差距，未定义被违反的合约是什么。

Method: 提出包含八部分（标识符、作用域、前置条件、后置条件、容差、参考预言机、测量协议、违规签名）的合约规范语言，定义十二种合约类别，要求三态校准（至少一个符合参考的实现和一个违反合约但通过基本功能测试的实现）。

Result: 将框架应用于华为Ascend静默精度截断、Sakana AI CUDA工程师奖励破解、AMD越界静默接受三个真实事件，证明每个非正式诊断都能映射到具体合约违规并具有可测量签名。

Conclusion: 内核合约套件可作为类似ISASecure对IEC 62443合规性分级的规范性参考，用于评估内核实现的符合程度。

Abstract: Every ML kernel ships with an implicit contract about what it computes. People rarely write the contract down. When two kernels disagree -- when a matmul on AMD produces a different gradient than the same matmul on NVIDIA, when a fused attention kernel silently downcasts an accumulator, when an out-of-bounds access returns zero on one stack and garbage on another -- there is no formal artifact to arbitrate the dispute. Recent empirical work has measured the gap across silicon platforms, but none of it specifies the contract being violated.
  We present a specification language for kernel contracts. A contract has eight parts: identifier, scope, precondition, postcondition, tolerance, reference oracle, measurement protocol, and violation signature. We use it to state twelve contract classes covering precision, ordering, compiler-induced, and exceptional-value failure modes, each grounded in published empirical evidence. We require a three-state calibration: every contract must admit at least one reference-conforming implementation and at least one contract-violating implementation that passes basic functional tests. We apply the framework to three documented incidents -- Huawei Ascend silent precision coercion, Sakana AI CUDA Engineer reward hacking, AMD out-of-bounds silent acceptance -- and show that each informal diagnosis maps to a specific contract violation with a measurable signature. A kernel contract suite is a normative reference against which conformance can be graded, in the way that ISASecure grades industrial control systems against IEC 62443.

</details>
