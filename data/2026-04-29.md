### [1] [Practical Insights into Fair Comparison and Evaluation Frame for Neutral-Atom Compilers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25478)
*Emil Khusainov,Yanbin Chen,Jonas Winklmann,Helmut Seidl,Christian B. Mendl*

Main category: cs.ET

TL;DR: 本文提出了一个统一且可复现的中性原子编译器评估框架RSQASM，通过标准化后编译表示消除评估不一致性，案例研究发现先前报告的多种编译器性能差异在消除不一致性后显著缩小甚至无法复现。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有中性原子编译器的评估指标基于工具链不同部分和非等价假设，导致公平量化和比较困难；报告指标可能因优化级别、运动时长模型、保真度来源不一致以及实现错误或文档缺失而不可靠。

Method: 提出RSQASM——一种受QASM启发的后编译表示，捕捉映射、路由和调度后的电路，包含显式并行门执行和穿梭操作；提供适配脚本将现有编译器输出转换为RSQASM；以HybridMapper、DasAtom和Enola三个工具链为案例进行重新评估。

Result: 多个先前声称的性能差距在消除评估不一致后显著缩小，部分情况下无法复现。

Conclusion: 统一的评估框架对于公平比较中性原子编译器至关重要，RSQASM提供了解决评估不一致问题的有效方案。

Abstract: Neutral-atom quantum computing is among the most promising platforms for scalable quantum computation, and compilation toolchains are crucial for leveraging capabilities such as qubit shuttling and parallel gate execution. An important challenge, however, is that existing neutral-atom compilers are often evaluated using metrics computed over different parts of the toolchain and under non-equivalent assumptions. Consequently, fair quantification and comparison of compiler performance remain difficult. Reported metrics may depend on inconsistent transpilation optimization levels, different movement-duration models, different sets of considered fidelity sources, and even minor implementation bugs or undocumented representation choices. To address this problem, we present a unified and reproducible evaluation framework for neutral-atom compilers. Our framework introduces RSQASM (Routed and Scheduled QASM), a QASM-inspired post-compilation representation that captures mapped, routed, and scheduled circuits, including explicit parallel gate execution and shuttling operations. As part of the framework, we provide adapter scripts that translate existing compiler outputs and intermediate artifacts into RSQASM. As a case study, we compare three well-known neutral-atom compilation toolchains: HybridMapper, DasAtom, and Enola, motivated by the large performance differences reported in prior work. Using our framework and representation, we perform a new evaluation and show that several previously claimed performance gaps become substantially smaller and, in some cases, are not reproduced once evaluation inconsistencies are removed.

### [2] [The Network Structure of Mathlib](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24797)
*Xinze Li,Nanyun Peng,Simone Severini,Patrick Shafto*

Main category: cs.LO

TL;DR: 对Lean 4的Mathlib库进行网络分析，揭示其依赖结构的宏观复杂性，发现人类设计的分类与逻辑结构存在50.9%的耦合，开发者仅使用导入范围的1.6%，网络中心性主要反映语言基础设施而非数学相关性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 理解大规模形式化数学库（Mathlib）的结构复杂性，探究逻辑、数学和基础设施依赖之间的关系。

Method: 将Mathlib的依赖结构提取为多层图（308,129个声明、840万条边、7,563个模块），通过图分解技术区分显式边、编译器合成边和证明驱动边，进行量化分析。

Result: 1) 人类设计的分类与逻辑结构之间存在50.9%的跨命名空间耦合；2) 开发者平均仅使用导入范围的1.6%；3) 网络中心性主要反映语言基础设施而非数学相关性。

Conclusion: 形式化数学的依赖结构具有独特属性，人类组织方式与逻辑结构存在显著偏差，且形式化过程压缩了语义层次。

Abstract: The ongoing development of Lean 4's Mathlib has produced a macroscopic structural complexity that interweaves logical, mathematical, and infrastructural dependencies. We present a network analysis of this library, extracting its dependency structure into a multilayer graph of 308,129 declarations, 8.4 million edges, and 7,563 modules. By introducing graph decompositions that isolate explicit edges from those synthesized by the compiler or driven by proofs, we quantify the structural properties of formalized mathematics. Our analysis reveals three findings. First, taxonomies designed by humans diverge from logical structures, exhibiting a 50.9% coupling across namespaces. Second, developers utilize a median of 1.6% of the imported scope. Third, formalization compresses semantic hierarchies, with network centrality capturing language infrastructure rather than mathematical relevance.

</details>
