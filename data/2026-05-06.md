### [1] [DITRON: Distributed Multi-level Tiling Compiler for Parallel Tensor Programs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02953)
*Size Zheng,Xuegui Zheng,Hanshi Sun,Qi Hou,Wenlei Bao,Shiyu Li,Haojie Duanmu,Jin Fang,Chenli Xue,Chenhui Huang,Yuanqiang Liu,Renze Chen,Ningxin Zheng,Dongyang Wang,Li-Wen Chang,Liqiang Lu,Yun Liang,Jidong Zhai,Xin Liu*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: DITRON是一个可扩展的tile级编译器，通过层级编程抽象（Core/Device/Task）实现分布式高性能内核开发，在NVIDIA和AMD平台上均达到或超过专家调优的CUDA库性能，已企业级部署并显著节省训练成本。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有分布式编程方案存在矛盾：高性能库（CuBLAS/NCCL）缺乏灵活性，无法适应快速演进的模型架构；而张量编译器又难以有效处理分布式集群的复杂内存层次结构。亟需一种既能提供高性能又能支持灵活分布式开发的解决方案。

Method: 提出了DITRON——一个可扩展的tile级编译器，核心创新是引入了层级编程抽象：Core级（核内计算）、Device级（设备内通信）、Task级（跨节点任务调度）。这一抽象将张量程序高效映射到异构分布式硬件上，同时支持多种并行策略并隐藏节点间/节点内通信的复杂性。

Result: 在大规模集群上，DITRON达到或超过专家调优的CUDA库性能：独立内核加速6%-30%，vLLM端到端推理加速5%-30%。训练任务MFU提升超10%，每月节省约50万GPU小时；推理任务端到端增益超20%，已部署于云服务和边缘推理场景。

Conclusion: DITRON成功弥合了高性能库与张量编译器之间的鸿沟，通过创新的层级抽象同时实现了高性能、灵活性和可移植性，证明了tile级编译能够在分布式环境中高效支持多样化的模型架构和硬件平台。

Abstract: The scaling of large language models (LLMs) is currently bottlenecked by the rigidity of distributed programming. While high-performance libraries like CuBLAS and NCCL provide optimized primitives, they lack the flexibility required for rapidly evolving model architectures. Conversely, existing tensor compilers fail to address the complex memory hierarchy of distributed clusters effectively. To bridge this gap, we propose DITRON, a scalable tile-level compiler that democratizes high-performance distributed kernel development. DITRON introduces a novel hierarchical programming abstraction spanning Core, Device, and Task levels to map tensor programs efficiently onto heterogeneous distributed hardware. This abstraction allows DITRON to support diverse parallelism strategies while abstracting away the complexity of inter-node and intra-node communication.
  Evaluated across large-scale clusters, DITRON achieves performance parity with or exceeding expert-tuned CUDA libraries, delivering speedups of $6\%-30\%$ on isolated kernels and $5\%-30\%$ on end-to-end inference in vLLM. Furthermore, DITRON demonstrates strong portability, achieving significant speedups on both NVIDIA and AMD platforms. \ours{} has been deployed at the enterprise level for both training and inference. It achieves an MFU improvement of over 10\% in training tasks, saving approximately 500,000 GPU hours of training cost per month. For inference tasks, it delivers an end-to-end gain of over 20\% and has been applied to cloud service inference and edge inference scenarios.

### [2] [Towards Definitional Interpreters for Hoare Logics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02963)
*Ke Sun,Di Wang,Yuyan Bao,Meng Wang,Dan Hao*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文探讨了如何在Rocq中实现基于Hoare逻辑推导（而非语法树）的内在定义解释器，提出了一种名为"entry-indexing"的新技术，用于解释全正确性推导和良基函数。研究成果包括：首次形式化了支持动态框架、良基函数、行为子类型和全正确性的Hoare逻辑，以及首个完全机械化的动态框架Hoare逻辑。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 借鉴内在定义解释器在类型推导上的成功经验，探索能否将同样的方法论应用于Hoare逻辑推导上，以利用类型安全性指导解释器构建的益处来保证程序正确性。

Method: 在Rocq中开发了两种定义解释器：(i)基础Hoare逻辑；(ii)支持堆、动态框架局部推理、良基函数和行为子类型的现实逻辑。核心创新是"entry-indexing"技术，用于解释全正确性推导和良基函数。

Result: 实现了首个形式化的动态框架Hoare逻辑（含良基函数、行为子类型和全正确性），以及首个完全机械化的动态框架Hoare逻辑。

Conclusion: 内在定义解释器的思路可以成功拓展到Hoare逻辑领域，entry-indexing技术有效支撑了全正确性推导的解释，为程序逻辑的机械化提供了新路径。

Abstract: Intrinsic definitional interpreters, definitional interpreters that operate on typing derivations instead of abstract syntax trees, have recently been studied as a promising methodology for defining dynamic semantics of programming languages. A key benefit is that type safety interactively guides and constrains the interpreter's construction. Analogously to typing relations, Hoare logic is widely used to guarantee program correctness. Can intrinsic definitional interpreters be realized to operate over Hoare-logic derivations?
  We explore this question in depth by developing definitional interpreters in Rocq for (i) a basic Hoare logic, and (ii) a realistic logic featuring heaps, dynamic-frame-based local reasoning, well-founded functions, and behavioral subtyping. Central to our approach is a novel technique we call entry-indexing, which we use to interpret total-correctness derivations and well-founded functions. Our second development yields, to our knowledge, the first formalization of a dynamic-frame-based Hoare logic with well-founded functions, behavioral subtyping, and total correctness, as well as the first fully mechanized Hoare logic with dynamic frames.

### [3] [Pact: A Choreographic Language for Agentic Ecosystems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03143)
*Kiran Gopinathan,Jack Feser,Michelangelo Naim,Zenna Tavares,Eli Bingham*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文提出了Pact，一种结合博弈论的编舞编程语言，用于在开放多智能体系统中设计自利智能体间的正确性协议，并实现了有界理性求解器来计算决策策略。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有的编舞编程虽能保证协议的"正确性"，但假设参与者完全合作，无法处理现实场景中智能体出于自利动机可能偏离协议的问题。

Method: 将编舞语言与博弈论结合，扩展出Pact语言：每个协议映射为形式化博弈，协议设计者可推理博弈论属性（如求解决策策略），并实现了有界理性求解器。

Result: 将Pact语言应用于多方协调场景，验证了其能够处理自利智能体参与的协议设计问题。

Conclusion: Pact为设计面向自利智能体、兼顾正确性与激励兼容性的多方协议提供了新范式。

Abstract: Recent advances in large language models have led to the rise of software systems (i.e. agents) that execute with increasing autonomy on behalf of users in open, multi-party settings, interacting with untrusted counterparts and managing private information. Choreographic programming offers correct-by-construction protocol-design for such settings, but assumes cooperative participants -- it has no notion of agent self-interest, that is, why an agent will follow a protocol.
  In this talk we introduce Pact, a choreographic language extended with operations to describe agent choices and preferences, drawing from the rich literature of game theory. Every Pact protocol maps to a formal game, allowing protocol designers to reason about game-theoretic properties of their protocols, such as solving for decision policies. We present Pact's design and a preliminary implementation -- a bounded-rational solver that computes decision policies over Pact protocols -- and findings from applying this language to multi-party coordination with self-interested agentic participants.

</details>
