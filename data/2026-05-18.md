### [1] [Polymorphic Bottom-Up Weighted Relational Programming](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15406)
*Dmitri Volkov*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种无需单态化即可在自底向上关系语言中实现多态性的新方法，基于semiringKanren语言和等式模式编译技术。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有自底向上关系语言在支持多态性时通常依赖单态化（monomorphization），这会导致代码膨胀和效率问题。本文旨在寻找一种更优雅、高效的编译方法来支持多态性。

Method: 1. 提出semiringKanren——一种自底向上加权关系编程语言作为基础；2. 扩展该语言以支持多态性；3. 设计基于等式模式和大规模多态关系实例的编译方法，将多态程序转换为非多态程序；4. 形式化证明该方法的正确性。

Result: 成功实现了一种无需单态化的多态编译方案，并通过正确性证明验证了方法的可靠性。

Conclusion: 该方法为自底向上关系语言的多态性实现提供了新思路，避免了单态化的缺点，未来可在此基础上探索更广泛的应用场景和优化方向。

Abstract: This work presents a new approach for implementing polymorphism for bottom-up relational languages, without monomorphization. We begin by introducing semiringKanren, a bottom-up weighted relational programming language. We extend this base language to support polymorphism. We describe a new method to compile polymorphic semiringKanren programs into non-polymorphic ones, based on equality patterns and large-enough instances of polymorphic relations. We prove the correctness of this method. Finally, we consider existing work and suggest directions for future research.

### [2] [Caesar: A Deductive Verifier for Probabilistic Programs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15827)
*Philipp Schröer,Kevin Batz,Umut Yiğit Dural,Darion Haase,Benjamin Lucien Kaminski,Joost-Pieter Katoen,Christoph Matheja*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: Caesar是一个用于概率程序的演绎验证器，基于HeyVL中间验证语言，可将概率程序、规范和证明规则以编程语言风格表达，并翻译为验证条件通过Z3 SMT求解器检查，同时包含基于概率模型检查的后端。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 为概率程序提供一种可扩展的演绎验证框架，使用户能够以编程语言风格轻松集成新的证明规则，并提升验证器的可用性和表达能力。

Method: 设计基于实值逻辑HeyLo的HeyVL中间验证语言，将概率程序、规范和证明规则统一表达为编程风格；翻译为验证条件后通过Z3 SMT求解器验证；同时针对HeyVL子集开发概率模型检查后端。

Result: 经过五年开发，Caesar实现了额外证明规则、模型检查后端和更好的诊断信息等改进。

Conclusion: Caesar通过HeyVL语言和双后端架构（SMT求解+模型检查），为概率程序验证提供了灵活、可扩展的解决方案。

Abstract: Caesar is a deductive verifier for probabilistic programs. At its core lies HeyVL, a quantitative intermediate verification language based on the real-valued logic HeyLo. HeyVL allows users to express a probabilistic program, its specifications, and proof rules in a programming-language style, so that new proof rules can be easily integrated into the verifier. Caesar translates HeyVL programs into verification conditions, which are then checked using the Z3 SMT solver. It also includes a backend based on probabilistic model checking for a subset of HeyVL. We report on the results of five years of development of Caesar, highlighting its main features and architecture. In particular, we describe recent improvements such as additional proof rules, a model-checking backend, and better diagnostics.

</details>
