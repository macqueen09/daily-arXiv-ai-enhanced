### [1] [From Rocq to Metal: A Pipeline for Formally Verified Microcontroller Firmware](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02651)
*Valentin Bergeron,Karolina Gorna*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文提出了Encore!，一个基于CPS的裸机虚拟机，能够在微控制器上运行Rocq提取的Scheme代码，实现形式化验证的固件。通过将固件结构化为纯状态转换函数，结合LLM辅助策略合成，让AI生成的固件能够自我证明正确性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 随着AI生成代码的普及，安全关键系统中不变量的强制执行日益紧迫，但支持高级规范语言的运行时对大多数嵌入式目标来说体积过大。

Method: 构建Encore!虚拟机（CPS风格、裸机运行），将固件设计为纯状态转换函数，使用Rocq进行形式化验证，并利用LLM辅助策略合成来生成形式化定理证明。

Result: 实现了可在微控制器上运行的形式化验证固件，未验证的宿主层保持恒定不变，不随固件复杂度增长。

Conclusion: 形式化定理陈述可以替代人工代码审查，使AI生成的固件能够自我证明正确性，为安全关键系统提供可行的形式化验证方案。

Abstract: Enforcing invariants in safety-critical systems is increasingly urgent as AI-generated code becomes widespread. Unfortunately, the runtimes required to support high-level specification languages are too large for most embedded targets. In this article, we show how formally verified firmware is achievable today. We built Encore!, a bare-metal Continuation Passing Style (CPS) virtual machine (VM) that runs Rocq-extracted Scheme on microcontrollers. We also show how to structure firmware as a pure state-transition function, making its core fully provable in Rocq while keeping the unverified host layer constant regardless of firmware complexity. Large Language Model (LLM)-assisted tactic synthesis fits naturally into this workflow: formal theorem statements replace manual code review, allowing AI-generated firmware to prove itself.

### [2] [Fixed-Point Scaffolding in the Clef Programming Language](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02854)
*Houston Haynes*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种基于范畴论和MLIR的编译器框架Composer，利用不动点组合子保留程序语义图中的维度、等级、逃逸和数值表示结构，通过函子从编译偏序集到目标范畴的构造实现代码降级与伴随验证，并引入紧致闭包负类型和分数类型，最终在MLIR工具链中实现结构保持的类型系统与证明项验证。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 在C++（通过MLIR）为ML族语言提供编译基础设施的情境下（呼应"Worse is Better"的讽刺），需要一种能够严格保持结构性（维度、等级、逃逸、数值表示）的正确性保证的编译方法，同时避免开发者直接面对范畴论复杂性。

Method: 使用不动点组合子保留PSG的结构信息；利用MLIR的SSA、属性系统和方言承载结构；采用范畴论构造（从编译偏序集到目标范畴的函子）实现降级及伴随验证；引入紧致闭包负类型和分数类型；通过Ohori的机器码证明论、参数性和伴随模态逻辑奠定三层理论基础。

Result: 证明了不动点原语既能保留类型结构也能提供证明项，这些证明项可在MLIR中持续验证降级管线的完整性；展示了类型机制可通过MLIR工具实现并保持结构；论证了该框架包含维度类型、Tarau's groupoid和cellular sheaves等独特贡献。

Conclusion: 该编译器框架提供了一个独特的基础，通过内部脚手架式的抽象（开发者无需直接使用范畴论）实现了形式化的编译保证，并且整个形式化体系被实现为编译器内部的支持结构。

Abstract: For fans of Gabriel's "Worse is Better" it may be ironic that C++, by way of MLIR, serves as the scaffold for compiling an ML-family language whose correctness properties are structural. A crucial intersection in our Composer compiler initiates its lowering with a fixed-point combinator that preserves the dimensional, grade, escape, and numeric-representation structure from the Program Semantic Graph. And the MLIR that's witnessed from the PSG is no passive host. Its use of static single assignment, attribute system and dialects carry that structure materially. We show that our compiler middle end uses categorical construction for lowering code with companion verification to that strata: a functor from the compilation poset to a target category, subject to the compositionality equation. The grounding of our approach comes from three sources, each on its own algebraic object: Ohori's machine-code proof theory grounds the compilation axis, parametricity grounds the content at the base, and adjoint mode logic grounds the traversal between our verification tiers. To extend the thesis we introduce compact-closed negative and fractional types, and show the type machinery can be carried with preserved structure and realized through tooling MLIR provides. More broadly, the same fixed-point primitive that preserves types through compilation also supplies proof terms that can continue to be exercised in MLIR to verify its integrity as lowering proceeds through the pipeline. We argue that this foundation is a unique additional point anticipated by our framework that includes dimensional types, Tarau's groupoid, and cellular sheaves. Throughout, the formalism is instrumented as an internal scaffold: the abstractions support the compiler's mechanics, where a developer is never required to reach for category theory in order to rely on the guarantees the compiler provides.

### [3] [Lean-GAP: A Dataset of Formalized Graduate Algebra Problems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02588)
*Seewoo Lee,Byung-Hak Hwang,Hyojae Lim,Jihoon Hyun,Ilkyoo Choi,Yeachan Park,Jineon Baek,Hyukpyo Hong,Keewoo Lee,Jaeseong Heo,Hyungryul Baik,Chul-hee Lee,Kyu-Hwan Lee*

Main category: cs.LO

TL;DR: 本文提出Lean-GAP，一个包含430道形式化研究生代数问题的数据集，开发了从PDF预处理到自动形式化再到验证的流水线，发现验证环节仍需要大量人工监督。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 将Dummit和Foote的《抽象代数》教材中的研究生级别代数问题进行形式化，填补大规模、高难度数学形式化数据集的空白。

Method: 构建了PDF-to-LaTeX预处理、自动形式化为Lean 4代码、以及非正式-形式化对应关系验证的三阶段流水线。

Result: 建立了包含430道形式化题目的结构化数据集；比较了不同自动形式化模型的性能；识别了形式化过程中的关键瓶颈。

Conclusion: 预处理和自动形式化可大幅自动化，但验证环节仍是最微妙、最劳动密集的部分，需要人工仔细监督。

Abstract: We present Lean-GAP (Lean-Graduate Agebra Problems), 430 formalized graduate-level algebra problems from the textbook Abstract Algebra by Dummit and Foote. We develop a scalable pipeline consisting of PDF-to-LaTeX preprocessing, autoformalization into Lean 4, and verification of informal-formal correspondence. While the preprocessing and autoformalization stages can be largely automated, we find that verification remains the most subtle and labor-intensive component, requiring careful human oversight. Our contributions include (i) the construction of a structured dataset of formalized exercises, (ii) a systematic methodology for formalizing textbook mathematics, and (iii) an analysis of recurring challenges in the formalization process. We also compare the performance of different autoformalization models and highlight key bottlenecks in translating informal statements into formal language.

</details>
