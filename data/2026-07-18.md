### [1] [Untrusted Authors, Trusted Answers: A Calculus of Fidelity-Graded Translations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14137)
*Christoph Kirsch*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文提出一种基于忠诚度分级的翻译演算，将翻译正确性从单次验证扩展到多语言、多路由的图结构，并通过Lean 4形式化实现。系统hurdy-gurdy包含13种语言和13对翻译，所有代码和论文由LLM生成，人类贡献仅在于架构设计。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统验证翻译的两个极端（认证编译和翻译验证）都只处理孤立的单次翻译，无法应对多源语言、多推理目标、多条独立路径构成的翻译图场景。本文旨在为这种复杂翻译图提供可组合的正确性保障机制。

Method: 提出忠诚度分级演算：1) 语言对之间的近交换方块可在每个程序上检查并通过粘贴组合；2) 声明的忠诚度等级通过最弱链接组合，每次运行通过内联检查重建；3) 独立派生路由之间的协议可超越声明等级；4) 端到端定理揭示了根本不对称性：携带见证的答案在源端自认证，而通用答案则需要等级、分支和证书来体现其成本。核心演算在Lean 4中机械化。

Result: 实现了hurdy-gurdy平台，包含13种语言和13对翻译，围绕两个推理中心构建。所有翻译对由独立LLM代理以无监督方式生成，架构交叉检查是唯一的语义门控。报告了联合覆盖率、分支一致性、合规基准、见证重放、认证不可达性和逃逸率实验。

Conclusion: 翻译图场景下，分级验证体系比单一验证方法更灵活可扩展。LLM生成代码+人类架构的模式可行，架构本身既是验证门控也是增长模型——任何人（使用LLM、代理或手工）贡献的语言对，由架构而非作者身份准入。

Abstract: Verified translation has two well-studied extremes: prove the translator once (certified compilation), or validate each run of one translator (translation validation). Both treat a single translation in isolation. We study translations as a graph -- many source languages, several reasoning targets, multiple independently built routes -- where the honest answer to "is this translation correct?" differs from edge to edge. We present a calculus of fidelity-graded translations: pairs of languages close commuting squares that are checkable per program and compose by pasting; declared fidelity grades compose by weakest link, are re-established per run by inline checking, and are exceeded by agreement between independently derived routes; and an end-to-end theorem isolates a fundamental asymmetry -- witness-carrying answers are self-certifying at the source, while universal answers are where grades, branches, and certificates earn their cost. The compositional core is mechanized in Lean 4.
  The calculus is implemented in hurdy-gurdy, a platform of 13 languages and 13 pairs around two reasoning hubs, built as a two-directional experiment in LLM-generated correctness: independent LLM agents wrote every pair, largely unsupervised, with the architecture's cross-checks as the only semantic gate, and the platform's intended player is itself an LLM. All code, and most of this paper, is LLM-generated; the human contribution is the architecture. The same gate is the intended growth model: hurdy-gurdy scales in language support through pairs contributed by anyone -- with LLMs, with agents, or by hand -- admitted by architecture, not authorship. We report conjoined coverage, branch agreement, a compliance-derived benchmark with machine-derived ground truth, witness replay, certified unreachability, and escape-rate experiments for the gate.

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有分布式系统中包含人及其个人设备，计算由人的意愿驱动（如支付、交友等），但缺乏一种既能表达机器状态又能表达个人意愿的编程语言和实现框架。

Method: 定义vGLP编程语言（扩展GLP添加意愿保护子句），将其操作语义定义为通信意愿代理的实例；通过AI将意愿保护的多智能体原子事务自动生成vGLP程序，再由AI将vGLP程序映射为包含UI构件的完整应用，部署到真实智能手机上。

Result: 在草根社交图、社交网络和数字货币三个平台上验证了该方法：每个平台均由AI从原子事务生成vGLP程序，再自动映射为部署在物理智能手机上的完整应用。

Conclusion: 实现此类系统需要引发人的意愿（即了解"人在想什么"），这是UI存在的唯一理由；vGLP通过意愿保护子句将人的意愿纳入计算语义，AI辅助的全流程生成证明了该方法的可行性。

Abstract: The most prevalent distributed systems today include people and their personal machines (smartphones). In such systems, computations are driven by people's volitions: a payment when a person wishes to pay someone, befriending when two people wish to become friends, etc. Volition-Guarded Multiagent Atomic Transactions were proposed as an abstract specification language for such systems, in which each agent consists of a person and their machine, and a transaction can be guarded by both the machine states and the personal volitions of its participating agents. Here, we define the programming language volition-guarded GLP (vGLP), which extends GLP with volition-guarded clauses, and define its operational semantics as an instance of Communicating Volitional Agents. As the semantics requires the person to will a volition-guarded clause reduction, a correct implementation must elicit the person's volitions: finding out ``what's in the person's head'' is the sole rationale for the UI, which is realised accordingly by standard constructs.
  We demonstrate the approach on the grassroots social graph, social network, and currencies: each platform is a vGLP program, generated by AI from volition-guarded multiagent atomic transactions; the implementation of vGLP, also created by AI, then maps its volition-guarded clauses into the user-interface constructs, resulting in a single working app deployed on a physical smartphone.

Main category: cs.PL

  <summary>Details</summary>
