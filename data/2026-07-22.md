### [1] [Decode-Time Grammars: Constrained LLM Generation over a Refinement Order of Grammar Fragments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18357)
*Shuoming Zhang,Ruiyuan Xu,Haofeng Li,Qiuchu Yu,Yangyu Zhang,Chunwei Xia,Xiaobing Feng,Chenxi Wang,Huimin Cui,Jiacheng Zhao*

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文提出解码时语法（decode-time grammars），通过从运行时环境实例化语法片段，在代码生成过程中动态约束引用合法性，消除对未定义符号的引用，在TileLang、SQL、P4等低资源编程面上实现零幽灵引用，且开销适中。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 大语言模型生成的代码越来越多地被直接编译或执行，缺乏人工审查，但主流语言之外的领域特定语言、自定义库API和命令行工具等低资源编程面仍存在生成非法引用的问题——即使使用语法约束解码，模型仍可能生成未声明的变量、不存在的列、库中未提供的函数或不支持的CLI选项。

Method: 提出解码时语法：运行时环境Gamma中实例化的语法片段。区域特定策略为每个"孔位"选择合适的片段，通过紧缩算子将开放引用位置替换为Gamma类型化的槽位，候选集为当前可用的名称、字段、API或选项。新生成的声明在后续区域解码前加入Gamma，使约束语法依赖于已生成的前缀。形式化地将语法片段定义为带精化偏序的环境索引语法，证明Gamma槽位片段的No-Ghost可靠性，并刻画掩码可强制属性的边界。实现为gproj系统，包含离线语法归纳和在线策略解析。

Result: 在TileLang、SQL和P4三个领域，使用0.6B到236B参数规模的模型，gproj在标准约束解码之上仅增加适度开销，从根本上消除了幽灵引用。

Newly generated declarations enter Gamma before later regions are decoded, so the constraining grammar can depend on the prefix already generated. This ensures not only grammatical correctness but also semantic correctness, by preventing references to undefined symbols.

We implement the approach in gproj with offline grammar induction and online policy resolution. Across TileLang, SQL, and P4, with models from 0.6B to 236B parameters, gproj eliminates ghost references by construction at moderate overhead over standard constrained decoding.

Main category: cs.PL

TL;DR: 本文首次实现了对乱序多处理器（out-of-order multiprocessor）相对于顺序、弱内存ISA的形式化无界验证。核心思想是通过精心设计的核规范（core specification）捕获超标执行（excess executions），并将证明分解为核精化（core refinement）和系统包含（system inclusion）两步，所有证明在Rocq中机械化，并利用LLM代理自动编写证明。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>

Method: 提出两个关键步骤：(1) 核精化（core refinement），证明核实现满足一个精心设计的核规范，该规范通过单个指令列表捕获超标执行的本质；(2) 系统包含（system inclusion），将乱序内存执行和核间交错序列化为ISA行为，利用核规范轻松消除超标执行。所有证明在Rocq中机械化，并借助LLM代理自动编写证明。

Result: 首次实现对乱序多处理器的形式化无界验证，将实现与顺序、弱内存ISA对应起来，所有证明均在Rocq中完成机械化。

Conclusion: 通过精心设计的核规范和两步分解证明策略，成功攻克了乱序多处理器在弱内存模型下的无界验证难题，展示了LLM代理在形式化证明中的辅助潜力。

The combination of the two yields weak outcomes, which no sequential execution explains, and modern ISA allows such behaviors to account for them. However, the microarchitecture even exhibits excess out-of-order executions, temporarily entering states forbidden by the ISA. While discarded later, such states complicate reasoning about the core in full-system verification. Prior works verify a range of processor designs, while none have performed unbounded verification for out-of-order multiprocessor exhibiting such weak outcomes.
  We present the first formal verification of an out-of-order multiprocessor against an in-order, weak-memory ISA. Our key idea is a well-designed core specification, which captures the essence of excess executions in a single list of instructions. Building upon this, we decompose the proof into two steps. The first is a core refinement, proving a core implementation against this specification, abstracting away every microarchitectural state except those necessary to reason about excess executions and the core interface. The second is a system inclusion, serializing the out-of-order memory executions and inter-core interleaving into the ISA, easily removing excess executions thanks to the core specification. All of our proofs are mechanized in Rocq, heavily utilizing large language model (LLM) agents to write proofs automatically.
